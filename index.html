The short version

I'm a student at the University of Washington's iSchool, studying Informatics with a Data Science focus (B.S., expected December 2027). Most days you'll find me somewhere between a spreadsheet and a sticky note: turning user research into product specs, or chasing down why a login funnel leaks users it shouldn't.

Outside of coursework, I'm building AlphaLens solo, serving as President of BINFO (Black in Informatics) at UW, and teaching Python to high schoolers through AVELA, who, more than anyone, remind me why any of this needs to be built responsibly in the first place.

That little pixel avatar running around above, Kai, is my digital twin: Black, Muslim, hijabi, mildly obsessed with Sudoku. She's been with me since I started thinking of this site less like a resume and more like a place worth exploring.
